Albert Einstein

Birthday: March 14, 1879

Who is he: Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist known for developing the theory of relativity, which revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. He is one of the most famous scientists in history and received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921. Einstein's work had a profound impact on the field of physics and our understanding of the universe. He was also a prominent advocate for civil rights and a pacifist.

Famous Quote: "Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world, stimulating progress, giving birth to evolution."

Where did he live: Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany, and spent a significant portion of his life in various places, including Munich, Prague, and Zurich. He later emigrated to the United States and lived in Princeton, New Jersey, where he continued his groundbreaking work.

Legacy: Albert Einstein's legacy extends far beyond his scientific contributions. He left a lasting mark as a humanitarian and scholar. His work continues to inspire scientific exploration and the pursuit of knowledge worldwide.
